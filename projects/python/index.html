Python projects

While I am quite familiar with Python, there's only one personal project in which I chose Python over another language. This was due to the large amount of library support for the task at hand.


papeScrape
papeScrape is a web scraper for the notorious imageboard 4chan. The name comes from the term "pape" used on /wg/ (4chan's wallpaper board), short for "wallpaper". The "scrape" part is self explanatory as it scrapes images from a thread of your choice.

As I am into system customisation on both desktop and mobile platforms, I often download wallpapers to change the look and feel of my system. It just so happens that 4chan's /wg/ board has a lot of good wallpapers being shared by other users. However, downloading an entire thread of good wallpapers can be tedious, so I finally had an excuse to try my hand at webscraping.

It didn't take long for me to feature creep my simple project to have a multitude of unplanned features:

  • Choose ANY board, not just /wg/.
  • Filter boards with NSFW content. Unfortunately /wg/ counts as NSFW as such content is permitted there.
  • Choose any thread within your chosen board, and relax as the script does the rest.
  • Multithreading support to allow for multiple concurrent downloads, further classifying this as a batch download tool.



The project was a success, and most of the features I wanted are in place. However, the controversial nature of the site the tool accesses does make me contemplate refactoring this tool for a more general purpose.
